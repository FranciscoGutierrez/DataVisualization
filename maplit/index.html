Introduction to the Map

This project is designed to provide the user with a unique perspective of 19th-century English fantasy horror by imposing both relevant historical events (e.g. The Whitechapel Murders) and significant references to London place names in 19th-century horror novels onto Charles Booth's 1889 map of London Criminality and Poverty. The result is a tool that helps us to understand the perceptions 19th-century readers and authors had about public spaces and the other in their contemporary London.

The works currently included in this visualisation are:
Robert Louis Stevenson's The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde
Bram Stoker's Dracula
Richard Marsh's The Beetle

At this time, the only crimes documented by the project are the 1888 Whitechapel Murders, conducted by Jack the Ripper.
